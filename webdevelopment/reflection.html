A Reflection

My goal is to build games and learning modules for cyber-security that are not only interactive but engaging. I want it to be said of my work that it was useful, and influential in the education of those who engage in the training I provide. My body of work continues to grow, but I want to know if the work I provide is useful and isnâ€™t numbered among the detritus that is churned out by the industry that surrounds IT and security. I continue to refine my work, and I accept all criticisms.

My work has thus far surrounded network-specific materials with cloud computing emphasis, but as AI continues to change the industry, I want to leverage those capabilities as I continue to work and integrate those capabilities into the training I produce. The world of instructional design will be furthered and expanded by AI generated content. Those who understand this now will be better positioned to offer quality materials later.

Cyber security at this point is a glut of companies that are hardly tech companies offering themselves as a cheap offering to the most willing buyers, and I find that professionally shameful. My career goals are more focused around established companies with established product offerings that are real. I have been a network engineer for 17 years and I want to expand into a role more aligned with solutions development and course offerings for technically challenging roles.

I have done some work in researching the efficacy of certifications, and I will also expand into that area to make a meaningful study of the certifications free of the influence of the certification industry. Much of that world has been plagued by the interests of an industry more concerned with the profitability of certification rather than its usefulness to the consumer. I hope this changes.
